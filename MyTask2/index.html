Grace Hopper: The Pioneer of Computer Programming

Grace Hopper was an American computer scientist and United States Navy rear admiral. She was one of the first programmers of the Harvard Mark I computer and developed the first compiler for a computer programming language. Her work laid the foundation for modern computing as we know it today.

Key Achievements

  • Developed the first compiler for a computer programming language.
  • Conceptualized the idea of machine-independent programming languages, leading to the development of COBOL.
  • Popularized the term "debugging" after fixing a computer glitch caused by an actual moth.

Famous Quote

"The most dangerous phrase in the language is, 'We've always done it this way.'"
